Claudia Margarita


Researcher & user advocate focused on how to make digital services more accessible and easy to use

Claudia Margarita Fernández Rivera is an award-winning designer, age-friendly policy and UX design researcher at the tPOT (towards People Oriented Technology) research group in TU Dublin.

She has a BA in Architecture, an Msc in Human Factors & Ergonomics and a Professional Diploma in Planning, Control and Management of Construction Projects.
She has been a practicing professional designer for 10 years and has a growing experience in the design of digital solutions.She has a keen interest in STEM, equality and education, age-friendly design and digital inclusion. Her policy development and research activities and wide-ranging volunteer work has been motivated by this interest and by the application of design as a democratising force.

Claudia is currently a funded researcher on behalf of TU Dublin on the EU_SHAFE project (INTERREG Europe), that seeks to influence regional and EU policy on smart healthy age-friendly environments (SHAFE) by sharing and transferring SHAFE best practices between participating member states and across European reference sites.

Her specific research topic focuses on the application of UX research to age-friendly digital public services.
